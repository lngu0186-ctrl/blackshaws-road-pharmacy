# Blackshaws Road Pharmacy — Comprehensive Website Audit
## April 5, 2026

---

## A. Executive Summary

**Overall Score: 73 / 100**

Blackshaws Road Pharmacy is a technically competent, visually polished website built on modern React + Vite + TypeScript. It demonstrates clear investment in design thinking and has evolved significantly from earlier iterations. The visual identity is strong, the routing is mostly clean, and the codebase is well-organised. However, there are meaningful gaps in conversion design, content depth, service page completeness, and technical polish that hold it back from competing at a chain-level standard.

The biggest strength is the **visual language and brand cohesion**. The biggest weakness is **thin content in service pages and underdeveloped health service detail pages** (all 27 `/health-services/*` routes point to a single placeholder). The most urgent priority is **expanding health service content pages** and **fixing SEO/meta issues on placeholder pages**.

**Biggest Strengths:**
1. Professional visual identity with consistent design system
2. Clean routing architecture with proper lazy-loading
3. Working Shopify integration for the online shop
4. Strong mobile navigation and mega menu
5. Good prescription upload flow with Supabase integration
6. Proper schema.org structured data for local business

**Biggest Weaknesses:**
1. 27 health service routes all render generic placeholder content (`noindex, nofollow`)
2. Missing meta descriptions on most page-level components
3. Contact form does not actually submit anywhere
4. No blog / health content strategy
5. Shop PDP lacks trust signals, detailed product info, and related merchandising

**Most Urgent Priorities:**
1. Replace HealthServicePlaceholder with real content for key service pages
2. Fix meta descriptions on individual pages
3. Connect contact form to a working backend endpoint
4. Remove `noindex` from the health services overview page (keep only individual placeholders)

## D. Detailed Findings

### 1. Brand / Visual Design — 84/100

**Strengths:**
- The navy/red/cream triad (`#10183f`, `#c0392b`, `#faf8f4`) is professional and distinctive. It avoids the green/cross pharmacy cliché entirely.
- Crimson Pro for display + Inter for body is a smart pairing with authority and warmth.
- The `section-label` component (uppercase red subheading with decorative rule) creates clear visual rhythm across pages.
- Cards use `rounded-[28px]` — a premium, contemporary treatment that feels distinctive.
- The phone mockup on the hero (when rendered) is a bold brand moment.

**Weaknesses:**
- The hero background image (`hero-pharmacy.png`, 1.2MB) is compressed to a WebP (122KB via preload) but the fallback PNG is still enormous. This is a risk for first-load performance.
- Inconsistency between sections: some pages use `section-padding` (variable `clamp(4.5rem, 8vw, 7rem)`), others use `section-padding-lg` (`clamp(5.5rem, 10vw, 9rem)`). This creates visual rhythm whiplash when a user moves between pages.
- The "BrandSignature" component (`src/components/layout/BrandSignature.tsx`) is injected on nearly every page hero, but its content may be repetitive without adding value.

### 2. Header / Navigation / Information Architecture — 78/100

**Current Structure:**
```
Top bar: Address, "Community pharmacy care, 7 days", Phone CTA
Main nav: Logo | Home | Prescriptions | Health Services ▼ | Plant Based Therapies | Compounding | Shop | FAQ | Contact | [Call] [Cart] [Book vaccination]
```

**Strengths:**
- 5-column mega dropdown for Health Services (27 items, grouped under: Preventive Health, Medication Services, Chronic Condition Support, Specialised Care, Life Stage & Wellness) is well-structured.
- "View all" link at top of mega menu is a smart convention.
- Mobile drawer has expandable service groups — a good pattern.
- Sticky header with backdrop blur is modern and doesn't obstruct content.

**Weaknesses:**
- `/health-services` items link out to pages that all render the **same placeholder** — this is misleading IA design. Users click "Health Checks" expecting specific content and get a generic template.
- "Plant Based Therapies" and "Compounding" appear both in the main nav AND in the Health Services mega dropdown. This is acceptable duplication but could cause confusion.
- No breadcrumb trail is visible on Shop pages or Product Detail pages, though the `Breadcrumb` component exists and is used on Shop.
- The "Call the pharmacy" button in the header has no `href` (just styled text) on desktop. This is a lost conversion.
- Cart badge shows `9+` for overflow — correct. But there's no cart preview drawer until after a 2-second delay (lazy loaded).

**This is the single biggest gap.**

```
/health-services/womens-health
/health-services/mens-health
/health-services/baby-breastfeeding
/health-services/skin-health
/health-services/uti-program
```

**Every single one** renders the same generic template:
```tsx
<p>Our pharmacists at Blackshaws Road Pharmacy provide {serviceName} support...</p>
<p>This information is general in nature...</p>
<Link to="/contact"><Button>Contact our pharmacy team</Button></Link>
<a href="tel:0393913257"><Button>Call (03) 9391 3257</Button></a>
```

And **every one** is set to `noindex, nofollow`, meaning **zero** of these 25+ pages are indexed by search engines.

**Exception:** `/plant-based-therapies` and `/compounding` are real, substantive pages with professional FAQ content. But they are the **only 2 out of 29** health-related service pages with unique content.

**There are actually service definitions** in `src/data/services.ts` — 29 individual services with full descriptions, pricing, "who is it for", "what to expect" — and a `ServiceDetail.tsx` component that renders them. But **these are completely disconnected** from the health services routes. The `/services` route redirects to `/health-services`, and `ServiceDetail.tsx` is never mounted.

This is a **major structural flaw** — there's an entire service content dataset, a working detail page component, and a navigation system, but the routes are pointing to a placeholder instead.

### 5. Prescription / Contact / Patient Flows — 58/100

**Prescription (/prescriptions):**
- The page is well-structured with pre-upload notes, a clear "How it works" section, and a call-to-action block.
- `UploadPrescription.tsx` integrates with Supabase for file uploads. It handles file type validation (JPEG, PNG, HEIC, WebP, PDF), max size (10MB), and patient data collection.
- Success/error states are handled properly.

**Contact (/contact):**
- The contact form in `Contact.tsx` **does not submit anywhere**. `handleSubmit` simply sets `submitted` to `true` and clears the form. No API call, no email dispatch, no webhook. This is a **dead form**.
- Opening hours are displayed.
- Google Maps embed is present with the correct address.
- Phone and address CTAs are clear.

**Patient Info (/patient-info):**
- A policy-style page covering prescription privacy, medicine availability disclaimers, and general health info disclaimers.
- Professional content, appropriate disclaimers. Fine as a supporting document.

### 6. Shop / eCommerce — 70/100

**Product Listing (/shop):**
- Fetches products from Shopify Storefront API. Works correctly.
- Category navigation maps Shopify types to pharmacy-specific categories (Pain & First Aid, Vitamins & Supplements, Baby & Toddler, etc.).
- Search, sort (featured, newest, price, A-Z), grid/list view, and pagination all work.
- Product cards show sale badges, out-of-stock states, and quick "Add" button.
- Mobile filter drawer is implemented.

**Product Detail (/shop/:handle):**
- Displays images, vendor, title, description (stripped of HTML), price, and an add-to-cart button with variant selection.
- Related products section renders items from the same category.
- Meta tags and schema are dynamically set per product.
- **Weakness:** PDP is thin. No ingredient/ingredients tab, no how-to-use, no reviews, no "Ask a Pharmacist" CTA, no shipping/delivery estimates, no "In stock" badge — just basic product info.

**Cart:**
- Uses Zustand store. Syncs with Shopify API.
- CartDrawer (lazy) shows items, updates quantity, removes items. Checkout URL links to Shopify's hosted checkout.

**Shopify Integration:**
- Token is loaded from `VITE_SHOPIFY_STOREFRONT_TOKEN` env var. The storefront API is the correct approach (client-safe).
- No hardcoded secrets in the repo.

### 9. Technical / Build / Code Quality — 76/100

- Supabase integration for prescription uploads.

**Weaknesses:**

- The Supabase URL and key are loaded from env vars — but if `src/integrations/supabase/client.ts` fails to resolve `VITE_SUPABASE_URL`, the app may crash at runtime.
- `CartStore` has an unused expression on line 245 — a minor logic issue.
- No `.env` file in the repo (correct for security), but `.env.example` is present with placeholder values.
- No CI/CD configuration (no `.github/workflows/`).

### 10. Accessibility — 64/100

**What's Present:**
- Skip-to-content link in `App.tsx`.
- Semantic HTML structure (`header`, `main`, `nav`, `footer`).
- `aria-label` on cart button and mobile menu toggle.
- `aria-expanded` on collapsible dropdowns and FAQ accordions.
- `loading="lazy"` on product images and map iframe.
- `decoding="async"` on product images.

**What Needs Work:**
- Focus states are not customised — default browser focus outlines may be insufficient.
- Form inputs in Contact and UploadPrescription use `placeholder` but should also have visible labels or `aria-label`.
- Colour contrast on the `text-white/78` and `text-white/74` tokens should be verified against WCAG AA (4.5:1 for body text). `rgba(255,255,255,0.74)` on a navy background may be borderline.
- No `prefers-reduced-motion` handling for animated elements (hero overlay, card hover transitions).

## H. Final Strategic Assessment

**What kind of website is this becoming?**

Blackshaws Road Pharmacy is on its way to being a **differentiated, content-capable independent pharmacy site**. It has the visual polish and routing architecture of a premium product. It avoids the clutter and advertising noise of Chemist Warehouse or Priceline. And it leans into Victorian Government programs (Chemist Care Now) in a way most independents do not.

**What does it still lack?**

1. **Content depth.** The 27 health services defined in `services.ts` are largely invisible to both users and search engines. The site needs individual landing pages for each service, optimised for local search intent.
2. **Human trust signals.** A pharmacy is a people business. There are no pharmacist profiles, no team photos, no "Our Story" page that genuinely tells the 1968-now evolution.
3. **Health content authority.** The site has no blog, no educational articles, no "Learn" hub. This means it cannot attract users searching for health information — only users searching for the pharmacy by name.
4. **Social proof.** No reviews, no testimonials, no Google rating widget. Trust is asserted but not proven.

**What would move it from "good independent" to "best-in-class local pharmacy digital experience"?**

1. **Service content at scale.** Every health service has its own SEO-optimised page with clear booking/contact CTAs.
2. **Pharmacist profiles.** Name, photo, qualification, specialty. This signals clinical capability.
3. **Health content engine.** A blog targeting local + general health queries, written in an approachable but professional tone.
4. **Chemist Care Now leadership.** Position Blackshaws Road as *the* Victorian pharmacy for pharmacist prescribing. Build a whole content cluster around it.
5. **Community storytelling.** Events, sponsorships, local school programs. This is the anti-chain advantage.

This site already competes visually with chains. It needs content, trust, and service depth to compete operationally. The foundation is strong. The next 8 weeks of work could make it exceptional.
